Adolf Hitler's Life

Adolf Hitler (1889–1945) rose from a failed art student to the dictator of Nazi Germany. After serving in World War I, he joined the German Workers' Party, eventually transforming it into the National Socialist German Workers' Party (Nazi Party). By 1933, Hitler was appointed Chancellor, and soon after became Führer, imposing a totalitarian regime that led to World War II and the Holocaust.

The Book: Mein Kampf

Mein Kampf ("My Struggle") was Hitler’s autobiographical manifesto, written during his imprisonment in 1924. It outlines his radical ideology including extreme nationalism, anti-Semitism, the concept of Aryan supremacy, and the desire for Lebensraum (living space). The book later served as the ideological foundation of the Nazi regime.

Understanding Fascism

Fascism is a far-right, authoritarian political ideology characterized by dictatorial power, forcible suppression of opposition, and strong regimentation of society. Nazi Germany was a fascist regime, but with a racial dimension, emphasizing Aryan supremacy and the exclusion of Jews and other minorities from society.

The Holocaust

The Holocaust was the state-sponsored, systematic persecution and murder of six million Jews by the Nazi regime and its collaborators. Victims also included Roma, disabled individuals, LGBTQ+ people, Slavs, and political opponents. Major events included the establishment of ghettos, mass shootings, and extermination camps like Auschwitz.
